# Contract Negotiation Playbook

Standard and fallback positions used to assess vendor/customer agreements.
The risk-analysis agent retrieves the relevant section to ground each clause.

## Limitation of Liability
Standard position: each party's aggregate liability is capped at the fees paid
in the trailing twelve (12) months, with consequential/indirect damages
excluded. Fallback: cap at 100% of total contract value. Dealbreaker: unlimited,
uncapped, or "not limited" liability, or a cap exceeding 2x annual fees.

## Indemnification
Standard position: indemnities are mutual and scoped to third-party claims for
IP infringement and breach of confidentiality, and are subject to the liability
cap. Red flag: one-sided or broad/uncapped indemnities that we grant the
counterparty.

## Confidentiality
Standard position: mutual confidentiality with reasonable-care obligations and a
survival period of three (3) years. A B2B contract must contain a confidentiality
clause; its absence is a critical gap.

## Termination
Standard position: termination for material breach on 30 days' uncured notice,
and termination for convenience on 60 days' notice. Avoid agreements we cannot
exit.

## Auto Renewal
Standard position: renewal in 12-month increments with at least 60 days' notice
of non-renewal. Policy limit: no auto-renewal term longer than 12 months and no
"evergreen" renewals without business-owner approval.

## Governing Law
Standard position: a neutral, well-understood jurisdiction (e.g. Delaware for US
deals, or the parties' shared jurisdiction). The contract must specify governing
law; its absence is a critical gap.

## Data Protection
Standard position: any processing of personal data must be governed by a Data
Processing Agreement compliant with GDPR Art. 28 and, for India, the DPDP Act,
2023. Red flag: personal-data processing with no DPA reference.

## Payment
Standard position: net-30 to net-45 payment terms; no unilateral price increases
mid-term beyond a capped annual percentage.

## Intellectual Property
Standard position: each party retains ownership of its pre-existing IP; we own
work product we pay for unless explicitly agreed otherwise.

## Dispute Resolution
Standard position: courts of the governing-law jurisdiction, or institutional
arbitration (e.g. SIAC/ICC) with a defined seat. Flag mandatory arbitration in an
inconvenient or counterparty-favourable forum.
